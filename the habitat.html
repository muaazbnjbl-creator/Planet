Habitats of the Strongest Forest Animals

Explore the natural homes of the most powerful animals and learn where they thrive in the wild.

Lion

Panthera leo

Lives in savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands of Africa, often near water sources.

Tiger

Panthera tigris

Inhabits tropical forests, mangroves, and grasslands across Asia, especially India and Southeast Asia.

Elephant

Loxodonta africana

Thrives in savannas, forests, and river valleys across Africa, often close to waterholes.

Bear

Ursus arctos

Found in forests, mountains, tundra, and coastal regions of North America, Europe, and Asia.

Leopard

Panthera pardus

Adapts to savannas, forests, mountains, and grasslands across Africa and Asia.

Jaguar

Panthera onca

Native to rainforests, wetlands, and riverine forests of Central and South America.

Gorilla

Gorilla beringei

Lives in dense tropical rainforests and mountain forests of Central Africa.

Crocodile

Crocodylus niloticus

Found in rivers, lakes, wetlands, and swamps across Africa, Asia, and Australia.

Hippopotamus

Hippopotamus amphibius

Resides in rivers, lakes, and swamps of sub-Saharan Africa, spending much time in water.

Rhinoceros

Rhinocerotidae

Lives in savannas, grasslands, and tropical forests of Africa and South Asia.
